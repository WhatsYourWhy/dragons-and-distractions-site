<section class="landing-section">
  <h2>What this is</h2>
  <div class="landing-grid">
    <article class="landing-card">
      <h3>An ADHD-led support toolkit</h3>
      <p>Dragons & Distractions is built around common executive-function pain points: overwhelm, time blindness, transition friction, shutdown, and distraction.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="landing-card">
      <h3>A way to name the pattern</h3>
      <p>Monsters make the problem easier to spot from the inside. The goal is recognition, relief, and action - not pretending the struggle is cute.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="landing-card">
      <h3>A practical shelf of tools</h3>
      <p>Every monster connects to rituals, printable aids, and plain-language moves you can actually try on a hard day.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="landing-section">
  <h2>What this is not</h2>
  <div class="trust-grid">
    <article class="trust-card">
      <h3>Not therapy</h3>
      <p>This site does not diagnose, treat, or replace professional care. If you are in crisis or need clinical support, use qualified medical or mental health resources.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="trust-card">
      <h3>Not a purity test</h3>
      <p>You do not have to relate to every monster, love the fantasy voice, or use every printable for the toolkit to be useful.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="trust-card">
      <h3>Not all-or-nothing</h3>
      <p>If one cue, one script, or one printed card helps you move, that counts. Small wins are the whole point.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>
