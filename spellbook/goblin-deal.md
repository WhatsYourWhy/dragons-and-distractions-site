    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>The deal</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>I see you, Goblin. Here is the deal: twenty minutes of work, then you get your shiny thing.</p>
        <p>The reward is chosen before the timer starts, not negotiated halfway through.</p>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>How to use it</h2>
      <ol>
        <li>Name the distraction out loud. Writing it down takes away half its power.</li>
        <li>Pre-choose the reward: a snack, a scroll, a song, a stretch. Decide now, not later.</li>
        <li>Set a short timer (15-25 minutes). Work the boring task until it rings.</li>
        <li>When the timer rings, take the reward. Fully. No guilt. Then decide: another round or done.</li>
      </ol>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>Why it helps</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>It stops the craving from being the villain and makes it part of the contract.</li>
        <li>It gives the brain a concrete finish line instead of open-ended suffering.</li>
        <li>It adds friction to the impulse without pretending the impulse is fake.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>Use it when</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>You opened one tab and lost the whole block, or the boring task feels physically harder than the distracting one.</p>
        <p>Rewards help, but only if you pick them before you start wandering.</p>
      </div>
    </section>
