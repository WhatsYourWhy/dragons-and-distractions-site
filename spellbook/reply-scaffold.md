  <p class="field-guide__lead">Use this ritual when Rejection Wisp has turned a single notification into a full-body alarm system. The point is not to feel brave about the reply. The point is to pick a shape, fill in the slots, and send the smallest safe version before your brain writes the novel about what it all means.</p>

  <div class="field-guide__grid">
    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>The rule</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>I choose the shape first. I fill the slots second. I send before I rewrite.</p>
        <p>A sent reply beats a perfect draft still open in seventeen tabs.</p>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>How to use it</h2>
      <ol>
        <li>Pick one reply shape: acknowledge, yes, no, or clarify.</li>
        <li>Fill the slots with the minimum honest words. No backstory, no apology tour.</li>
        <li>Read it once for facts. If the facts are right, send it.</li>
        <li>Close the thread. You replied. The rest is not your job right now.</li>
      </ol>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>Why it helps</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>It removes the blank-page problem from emotional replies.</li>
        <li>It separates "what do I say" from "what does this mean about me."</li>
        <li>It lets you act before the Wisp finishes narrating.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>Use it when</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>You know what the reply should say, but typing it feels like defusing a bomb.</p>
        <p>One awkward thread is poisoning every other unopened message.</p>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--wide">
      <h2>Minimal prompt</h2>
      <pre class="field-guide__prompt"><code>Reply Scaffold

Send.</code></pre>
