  <p class="field-guide__lead">Use this ritual when Sensory Storm has your nervous system maxed out and someone is about to suggest a productivity app. The point is not to power through the noise. The point is to lower the loudest input first and see how much capacity comes back on its own.</p>

  <div class="field-guide__grid">
    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>The rule</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>Subtract before you add. Lower one input before you reach for another tool.</p>
        <p>Shelter first, strategy second.</p>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>How to use it</h2>
      <ol>
        <li>Name the loudest, brightest, scratchiest, or most socially demanding input right now.</li>
        <li>Lower or remove that one thing. Headphones off, lights down, door closed, notification silenced.</li>
        <li>Give your body one steady anchor: cold water, pressure, a single calm sound, or dimmer light.</li>
        <li>Wait two minutes. Then ask what your brain can handle from here.</li>
      </ol>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>Why it helps</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>It treats overload as an input problem, not a willpower problem.</li>
        <li>It works faster than any strategy that adds more things to manage.</li>
        <li>It gives the nervous system room to come back online before you ask it to perform.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>Use it when</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>You are getting fragile, foggy, or snappy faster than the situation seems to justify.</p>
        <p>Changing the environment helps more than another productivity trick.</p>
      </div>
    </section>
