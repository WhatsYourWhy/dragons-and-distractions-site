<div class="field-guide">
  <p class="field-guide__lead">Use this ritual when Perfection Wyrm has you editing paragraph one for the third hour while paragraphs two through ten do not exist yet. The point is not to write badly on purpose. The point is to let the whole shape exist before any part of it gets polished.</p>

  <div class="field-guide__grid">
    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>The oath</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>My first job is existence, not elegance.</p>
        <p>I will make the whole shape before I polish any sentence.</p>
      </div>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>How to use it</h2>
      <ol>
        <li>Set a timer for the entire draft, not the first section.</li>
        <li>Write every section as bullet points, placeholders, or garbage prose. All of it counts.</li>
        <li>Mark anything that feels wrong with [FIX LATER] and keep moving.</li>
        <li>When the timer ends, you have a full ugly shape. Now you may polish.</li>
      </ol>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card">
      <h2>Why it helps</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>It separates creation from editing, which use different brain modes.</li>
        <li>It removes the Wyrm's leverage: you cannot endlessly revise what does not exist yet.</li>
        <li>It makes "done" visible before "perfect" gets a vote.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section class="field-guide__card field-guide__card--quote">
      <h2>Use it when</h2>
      <div class="field-guide__quote">
        <p>You have rewritten the opening three times and the rest of the thing is still blank.</p>
        <p>Finishing feels riskier than another round of edits.</p>
      </div>
    </section>
